Germany's technology market is the largest in Europe, valued at over €120 billion in 2024. The country is a leader in Industry 4.0, automotive technology, and artificial intelligence adoption. Berlin has emerged as Europe's fastest-growing startup hub, with over 1,500 tech startups and a venture capital investment of €7.8 billion in 2023. The German government has committed €10 billion to AI research and development through its "AI Made in Germany" initiative. Munich, Stuttgart, and Hamburg are also major tech hubs, housing companies like SAP, Siemens, and numerous autonomous vehicle startups. The country is investing heavily in quantum computing, with a €2 billion plan to develop Europe's first quantum computer by 2028. Germany faces challenges including a shortage of skilled tech workers, with over 150,000 open IT positions in 2024. The government is implementing a new skilled worker immigration law to attract international tech talent, with simplified visa procedures and a points-based system. Cloud computing, cybersecurity, and green technology are the fastest-growing sectors, with projected annual growth rates exceeding 15% through 2027. The German tech market is expected to reach €150 billion by 2026, driven by digital transformation across all industries and increased public-private partnerships in technology innovation.
